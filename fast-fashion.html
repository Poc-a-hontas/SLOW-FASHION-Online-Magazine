Not sure about the definition of fast fashion? Cheap and trending clothes that get
produced rapidly and in a huge amount. Basically, the idea behind it is to get the newest
trends from catwalks to the people in as little time as possible. Sadly, we are still living
in a world where wearing the same piece of clothes repeatedly is considered a faux-pas,
therefore we are somewhat pressured to buy new clothes very often and throw them away
after just a few times of wearing it, also known as a ‘throw-away culture’. Sick, I know.

THE TRUE COST

About the equivalent of one truck full of clothes is being
burned or dumped in a landfill every single second.
Moreover, fashion industry is the second largest polluter
of clean water globally (right after agriculture).
Brands such as H&M or Zara very often use cheap labour
in order to reduce the cost and that way gain more profit.
These workers work for very little money, in dangerous environments
as they are in a direct contact with toxic elements,
with no human rights whatsoever. So next time you go to a mall,
ask yourself twice whether you need a new T-shirt for such cost.

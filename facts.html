Now, try looking at the clothes you are wearing right now. What do you know about its environmental impact?
Who made it? Were they paid enough? For many brands, transparency and sustainability is still a taboo.
Here are ten facts about fashion industry (careful, it will probably make you feel guilty)

1

Over 100 billion items of clothing are produced each year.

2

93% of fashion brands are not paying workers a living wage.

3

Textile production contributes to climate change more than international aviation and shipping combined.

4

The average American throws away around 35kg of clothing yearly.

5

Over 7 000litres of water are needed to produce a single pair of jeans (that’s about the amount of water a person drinks in 5 years).

6

The fashion industry makes 3 trillion dollars yearly. The workers work for less than $3 a day.

7

80% of garment workers are women. (and no, that is not female emancipation, but exploitation).

8

Washing a load of synthetic clothes can release more than 700, 000 micro-plastics.

9

Luxury brands such as Louis Vuitton or Chanel burn their unsold collections rather than going for discounts as they claim that would 'hurt' their image.

10

There are no longer new collections four times a year (as one for each season), fast fashion brands are able to make 52 new collections a year- one for each week of the year.









But let’s not only blame the brands. After all, we all vote with our dollars. Think about it.
